“夏川，0分！”
四十人的教室内，老师的宣告像火一样点燃了台下同学们的激情，好奇地盯着被审判的那位少年——夏川。
夏川被盯着也不孬，举起手就打算问个缘由。
“黄老师，我对过答案了，怎么可能是0分”夏川说。
“这正是我接下来准备说的了，你们看看这张卷子”老师说着，把手上的卷子投在了大屏幕上。
“星澈，你告诉同学们问题在哪”
老师看向了夏川的同桌，后者站了起来回答道。“没写名字”
“夏川，听到了吧，虽然你这次英语成绩挺不错，但是名字都不写，写的再好老师给只能给你0分，记得引以为戒”
夏川尴尬的挠挠头，决心认真听课弥补之前的错误，然而15分钟后，不知不觉夏川已经闭上了眼睛。
还好星澈在旁边用笔戳了下，将这节课的听课时间延长到了20分钟。
"Xia Chuan, zero marks!"

In the classroom of forty students, the teacher's declaration ignited the excitement of the classmates like a flame, as they all stared curiously at the boy being judged—Xia Chuan.

Even under their gaze, Xia Chuan remained unfazed. He raised his hand, intending to ask for an explanation.  

"Teacher Huang, I checked the answers. How could I possibly get zero marks?" Xia Chuan said.  

"This is exactly what I was about to explain next. Everyone, take a look at this test paper," the teacher replied, projecting the paper onto the large screen.  

"Xing Che, please tell the class what the problem is."  

The teacher turned to Xia Chuan's deskmate, who stood up and answered, "He didn't write his name."  

"Xia Chuan, did you hear that? Although your English performance this time was quite good, if you don't even write your name, no matter how well you did, the teacher can only give you zero marks. Remember to learn from this," the teacher said.  

Xia Chuan scratched his head in embarrassment, determined to focus on the lesson to make up for his earlier mistake. However, fifteen minutes later, he had unknowingly closed his eyes.  

Fortunately, Xing Che beside him poked him with a pen, extending his time spent listening in class to twenty minutes.
下课铃响后，夏川立刻倒头就睡，只是他似乎忘了些什么。
直到下一节课的来临，他醒来，就看到自己的同桌看向自己。
“自己的发型好像乱了”这是夏川的第一想法。
然而后者不理会夏川的窘迫，只是把一张卷子拿给了他。
“94分”
“什么？”
“我上课把答案对完了，除了写作题以外，你这次的成绩是94分，算上作文应该能上120以上”
“谢…谢谢”
“没什么，毕竟我是课代表。”
夏川惊喜自己的成绩原来这次这么高，不过却没注意到旁边的星澈不自觉的把一张试卷放在抽屉里。
这节课是数学课，也是上午最后一节课，因为最近举办了月考，所以这周的课都是以将月考题目复盘为主。
数学是夏川的强项，他喜欢这些小东西，可能是高一的缘故，考的都是些简单的题目，不过对其他人就不一定了，复杂的数学概念对他人来说犹如天书。
很快，数学课结束，下课铃声响起，万马奔腾。
很多人都加快步子向校门走，毕竟晚了就要排很长的队了。在三中，是允许高中生走读的，只不过需要用学生卡刷门禁才能出去，唯二的两个门不可避免地导致拥堵。
夏川也跑了出去，他坐在教室右前方，离门很近，不过他中途又回头看了一眼星澈，她似乎并不着急，仔细想来，开学那么久，星澈总是一个人坐着，偶尔有几个人找她但也是老师。
“算了，这次就在校内吃吧”夏川喃喃道，然后前往了校内食堂。回到家也没人等他，只是自己做饭，偶尔也给自己放松一下。
After the bell rang, Xia Chuan immediately slumped over the desk and fell asleep, though he seemed to have forgotten something.  

By the time the next class arrived and he woke up, he saw his deskmate looking at him.  

“My hair seems messy” was Xia Chuan’s first thought.  

However, the other person ignored his embarrassment and simply handed him a test paper.  

“94 points.”  

“What?”  

“I checked all the answers during class. Apart from the writing section, your score this time is 94. Including the essay, you should be able to get over 120.”  

“Th… thank you.”  

“No need. After all, I’m the class representative.”  

Xia Chuan was delighted to learn that his score was so high this time, but he didn’t notice that Xing Che had unconsciously placed another test paper inside her desk drawer.  

This period was math class, the last one before noon. Since the monthly exams had recently been held, this week’s lessons were mainly focused on reviewing the exam questions.  

Math was Xia Chuan’s strong suit—he enjoyed these little puzzles. Perhaps because it was only the first year of high school, the questions were still relatively simple, though for others, it might not be the same. Complex mathematical concepts seemed like an indecipherable language to many.  

Soon, the math class ended, the bell rang, and it was as if a herd of horses had been unleashed.  

Many students quickened their pace toward the school gate, knowing that leaving late would mean standing in long lines. At No. 3 High School, high school students were allowed to commute from home, but they needed to use their student cards to swipe through the gate to leave. With only two gates available, congestion was inevitable.  

Xia Chuan also rushed out. He sat near the front right of the classroom, close to the door, but halfway there, he glanced back at Xing Che. She didn’t seem to be in a hurry. Thinking about it carefully, since the start of the semester, Xing Che had always been sitting alone, with only a few people approaching her—mostly teachers.  

“Forget it, I’ll just eat at the school cafeteria today,” Xia Chuan murmured to himself before heading toward the school cafeteria.
“……”
“……”
“……”
“这个，这个，还有这个！那个饭再加点！”
夏川经过拥堵的人群，来到任务点，成功获取了筷子和汤。然后找了个空位坐了下来。
“人可真多……”夏川感慨道，然后开始享用午餐。
与此同时，另一边。
星澈只是等人走的差不多后，从书包里默默取出面包果腹。她看了看旁边的空位，想起了某个人，笑了。
这时，突然有个人从门口进来，吓了她一跳。
“苏云”星澈不经意地看了看对面的脸，是个女生，看到是同班同学，她没有在意，借着书垒起地高墙遮掩，然后，继续吃面包。
突然，寂静地教室出现了人声。
星澈好奇向左侧声音来源处望去，原来是苏云在用什么东西和人通话，看样子像是电话手表。
虽然偷听别人聊天不好，但教室就这么大，不可避免会听见别人讲话。
星澈发现苏云的声音从开始的平稳，变得有些急躁，不对她的身体好像不太对劲，摇摇晃晃的。
“都说了，我在校医看过了，需要请假去市医院进一步检查。”
“学习是很重要，可是我就不重要吗！”
咚——
教室重归安静，看样子是对方把电话挂了。
看着苏云的样子，星澈很不是滋味，想过去开导几句。这时夏川也吃饱喝足来到了教室。
突然，苏云双手紧紧抓着窗户上的防盗网，重心迅速向下，直到倒在地上。
两人都发现情况不对，靠近一看，苏云摔倒在地上。
“星澈，你扶着苏云，我去找老班”
星澈点点头，把苏云的一个胳膊架在自己脖子上，打算让她先坐下来好好休息一下。
很快，班主任赶来了，确认情况属实后，立刻拨打了120，并让校医赶来做初步诊断。
"This, this, and this! And add a little more rice to that one!"

Xia Chuan pushed through the crowded crowd, arrived at the serving area, successfully retrieved his chopsticks and soup, and then found an empty seat to sit down.  

"There really are a lot of people..." Xia Chuan sighed, then began to enjoy his lunch.  

Meanwhile, on the other side.  

Xing Che waited until most people had left before quietly taking out a bread roll from her bag to eat. She glanced at the empty seat beside her, thought of someone, and smiled.  

Just then, someone suddenly walked in through the door, startling her.  

"Su Yun," Xing Che inadvertently glanced at the face opposite her—a girl. Seeing that it was a classmate, she didn't pay much attention. Using the high wall of books as cover, she continued eating her bread.  

Suddenly, the quiet classroom was filled with the sound of a voice.  

Curious, Xing Che looked toward the source of the sound on her left. It turned out Su Yun was talking to someone using something that looked like a smartwatch.  

Though it wasn't polite to eavesdrop on others' conversations, the classroom was small, making it unavoidable to hear what others were saying.  

Xing Che noticed that Su Yun's voice, which had been steady at first, was becoming somewhat agitated. No, something seemed wrong with her—she was swaying unsteadily.  

"I already said, I went to the school clinic. I need to take leave to go to the city hospital for further checks."  

"Studying is important, but am I not important too?"  

Thud—  

The classroom fell quiet again. It seemed the other person had hung up.  

Seeing Su Yun's state, Xing Che felt uneasy and wanted to go over to offer a few words of comfort. Just then, Xia Chuan, having finished his meal, also returned to the classroom.  

Suddenly, Su Yun grabbed the window's security bars tightly with both hands, her center of gravity rapidly dropping until she fell to the ground.  

Both of them realized something was wrong. Approaching for a closer look, they found Su Yun collapsed on the floor.  

"Xing Che, you help Su Yun. I'll go find the head teacher."  

Xing Che nodded, placing one of Su Yun's arms over her shoulder, intending to help her sit down and rest properly.  

Soon, the head teacher arrived. After confirming the situation, they immediately called 120 and asked the school nurse to come for a preliminary diagnosis.<cont>
